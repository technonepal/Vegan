Animal agriculture is a major contributor to greenhouse gases, deforestation, and water use. Shifting to vegan diets can cut food-related emissions by up to 70%. Vegan diets result in 75% less climate-heating emissions than meat-heavy diets.

Activists like Mike Hudema, a climate campaigner with Greenpeace, have repeatedly highlighted the links between animal agriculture and the climate crisis through advocacy for green solutions and reduced fossil fuels. His work emphasizes structural changes that align with plant-based shifts to combat global warming.
