The concept of avoiding animal products dates back over 2,000 years to ancient Greek philosophers like Pythagoras. The term "vegan" was coined in 1944 by Donald Watson, founder of The Vegan Society.

Early evidence traces to 9,000 years ago in the Indus Valley, with plant-based practices in ancient India and the Mediterranean. Roman philosopher Seneca adopted vegetarianism influenced by Pythagoras.
